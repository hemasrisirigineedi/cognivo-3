Introduction to Kinetic Theory

Kinetic Theory explains the macroscopic behavior of gases (pressure, temperature, volume) using the microscopic motion of molecules.

It connects:

Molecular motion ⟶ Pressure

Molecular kinetic energy ⟶ Temperature

2️⃣ Postulates of Kinetic Theory (VERY IMPORTANT)

An ideal gas is assumed to satisfy the following:

Gas consists of a very large number of molecules

Molecules are in continuous random motion

Size of molecules is negligible compared to distance between them

Intermolecular forces are negligible except during collisions

Collisions between molecules and with walls are perfectly elastic

Gas molecules obey Newton’s laws of motion

Time of collision is negligible compared to time between collisions

📌 JEE Tip: Any violation → gas becomes real, not ideal.

8️⃣ Degrees of Freedom (DOF)

Degrees of freedom = number of independent ways a molecule can store energy.

Gas	DOF (f)
Monatomic	3
Diatomic (room temp)	5
Triatomic (linear)	5
Triatomic (non-linear)	6

📌 At high temperature, vibrational DOF becomes active.
