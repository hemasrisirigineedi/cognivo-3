1️⃣ Introduction to Force and Motion
Concept

Motion of an object changes only when a force acts on it.

Force can:

Change speed

Change direction

Change shape

Start or stop motion

Example

Kicking a football makes it move

Applying brakes stops a moving car

2️⃣ Force
Definition

A force is a push or a pull that can change the state of motion or shape of an object.

SI Unit

Newton (N)

Example

Pushing a door to open it

Pulling a drawer

3️⃣ Balanced and Unbalanced Forces
Balanced Forces

Forces that cancel each other → no change in motion

Unbalanced Forces

Forces that do not cancel → motion changes

Example

Tug of war with equal force → rope doesn’t move (balanced)

One team stronger → rope moves (unbalanced)

4️⃣ Newton’s First Law of Motion (Law of Inertia)
Statement

An object remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted upon by an external unbalanced force.

Inertia

The tendency of an object to resist change in its state of motion.

Types of inertia:

Inertia of rest

Inertia of motion

Inertia of direction

Example

Passengers jerk backward when a bus starts suddenly

Passengers jerk forward when a bus stops suddenly

8️⃣ Newton’s Third Law of Motion
Statement

For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Important Points

Action and reaction act on different bodies

They are equal in magnitude and opposite in direction

Example

Walking: foot pushes ground backward, ground pushes foot forward

Rocket propulsion

🔟 Equilibrium of a Particle
Concept

When net force on an object is zero, it is in equilibrium.

Conditions:

At rest

Or moving with constant velocity

Example

A book resting on a table:

Gravity downward

Normal force upward

Net force = 0

1️⃣5️⃣ Pseudo Force (Non-Inertial Frame)
Concept

When observed from an accelerating frame, an imaginary force appears.

Example
