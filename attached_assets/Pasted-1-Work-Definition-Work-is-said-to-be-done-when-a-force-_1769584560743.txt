6. Conservative and Non-Conservative Forces
Conservative Force

Work done depends only on initial and final position

PE can be defined

Examples:
✔ Gravitational force
✔ Spring force

Non-Conservative Force

Work depends on path

PE cannot be defined

Examples:
✖ Friction
✖ Air resistance

9. Collisions
Definition

When two bodies interact for a short time and exchange momentum.

Types of Collisions
Elastic Collision

Momentum conserved

Kinetic energy conserved

Example:
✔ Billiard balls

Inelastic Collision

Momentum conserved

Kinetic energy NOT conserved

Example:
✔ Car accident

Completely Inelastic Collision

Bodies stick together after collision

10. Momentum Conservation (in collisions)
Law

Total momentum before collision = total momentum after collision

Example

Gun recoil:

Bullet moves forward

Gun moves backward
Total momentum remains zero.
