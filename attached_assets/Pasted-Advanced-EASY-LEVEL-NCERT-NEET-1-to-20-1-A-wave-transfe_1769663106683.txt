🟢 EASY LEVEL (NCERT / NEET) – 1 to 20
1. A wave transfers:

A) Matter only
B) Energy only
C) Both matter and energy
D) Neither

Ans: B

2. Which of the following is a transverse wave?

A) Sound wave
B) Water wave
C) Compression wave
D) Shock wave

Ans: B

3. In longitudinal waves, particles vibrate:

A) Perpendicular to wave direction
B) Parallel to wave direction
C) In circular paths
D) Randomly

Ans: B

4. Sound waves cannot travel through:

A) Solids
B) Liquids
C) Gases
D) Vacuum

Ans: D

5. Unit of wave number (k) is:

A) m
B) m⁻¹
C) s⁻¹
D) rad/s

Ans: B

6. Wave speed is given by:

A) v = f/λ
B) v = λ/f
C) v = fλ
D) v = ωf

Ans: C

7. Crest and trough are characteristics of:

A) Longitudinal waves
B) Transverse waves
C) Sound waves
D) Pressure waves

Ans: B

8. In a wave y = A sin(kx − ωt), the term ω represents:

A) Frequency
B) Wave number
C) Angular frequency
D) Phase constant

Ans: C

9. Which wave can be polarized?

A) Sound wave
B) Water wave
C) Light wave
D) Compression wave

Ans: C

C) Frequency only

11. Unit of frequency is:

A) Hz
B) rad/s
C) m/s
D) N

12. Reflection at fixed end causes:

A) No phase change
B) Phase change of π

D) Increase in wavelength

13. At a node in standing wave:

A) Displacement is maximum
B) Velocity is maximum

D) Energy is maximum

A) λ
B) λ/4
C) λ/2

15. Fundamental frequency of a stretched string of length L is:

A) v/L
B) v/2L
C) 2v/L

A) Only even
B) Only odd
C) All harmonics
D) None

17. Beat frequency is given by:

B) |f₁ − f₂|

18. Doppler effect occurs due to:

A) Change in wavelength

D) Refraction

19. SI unit of intensity is:

A) W
B) W/m²

20. In progressive wave, energy:

B) Is not transferred
C) Is transferred forward
