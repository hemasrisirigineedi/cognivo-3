1пёҸвғЈ Introduction to Motion

Motion is the change in position of an object with time relative to a reference point.

If position changes вҶ’ object is in motion

If position does not change вҶ’ object is at rest

рҹ“Ң In this chapter, we study rectilinear motion (motion along a straight line).
рҹ“Ң Objects are treated as point objects when their size is very small compared to distance travelled.
рҹ“Ң Kinematics describes motion without discussing the causes (forces).

рҹ”№ Example

A car moving from 0 m to 50 m in 5 s is in motion because its position changes with time.
