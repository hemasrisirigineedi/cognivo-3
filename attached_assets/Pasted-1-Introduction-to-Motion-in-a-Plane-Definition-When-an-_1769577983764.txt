1️⃣ Introduction to Motion in a Plane
Definition

When an object moves in two directions at the same time, its motion is called motion in a plane (2-D motion).

In this type of motion, we need two coordinates (x and y) to describe the position.

Example

A ball thrown at an angle

A car taking a turn

An airplane flying diagonally

All these move in two directions simultaneously.

2️⃣ Scalars and Vectors
Scalar Quantity

A scalar has only magnitude, no direction.

Examples:

Distance (5 m)

Speed (10 m/s)

Time (2 s)

Mass (50 kg)

👉 Scalars tell “how much” only.

Vector Quantity

A vector has both magnitude and direction.

Examples:

Displacement (5 m east)

Velocity (20 m/s north)

Acceleration (9.8 m/s² downward)

👉 Vectors tell “how much” and “which direction”.

5️⃣ Equality of Vectors
Definition

Two vectors are equal if:

Their magnitudes are equal

Their directions are same

Example

Two arrows of length 5 cm pointing east are equal vectors, even if drawn at different places.

Example
